Key — Abbreviations Used
Sizing note: Portex, Tracoe, Shiley Flexible, and Bivona tubes all use ISO sizing — the tube size number equals the inner diameter of the inner cannula in mm (e.g. size 8 = 8.0 mm ID-IC). Shiley Standard/DCT series uses older Jackson sizing and is not ISO — size 8 = 7.6 mm ID. Always check which series is in use.
ID-IC Inner diameter at bottom of inner cannula (mm) — the effective airway lumen
OD-OC Outer diameter of outer cannula (mm) — determines tracheal fit
TL Tube length along centre line from flange to tip (mm)
OB Length along outer bend from flange to tip (mm)
BA Bending angle in degrees (°) — angle of the tube curve
CD Cuff inflated diameter (mm)
IC Inner cannula — inner tube that can be removed for cleaning/replacement
SSH / Suctionaid Subglottic suction port — allows drainage of secretions above the cuff

Tracoe (Atos Medical)
Tracoe Twist
Polyurethane (PU) outer cannula · Double-swivel neck flange · Low-pressure PVC cuff
Source: Official Atos Medical / Tracoe Medical technical data sheet MC3043-TcEN 202306 — directly from atosmedical.com. All measurements verified.
Clinical notes: Sizing corresponds to the ID of the inner cannula (ID-IC), not the outer cannula lumen. The double-swivel neck flange rotates on two axes. Thinner tube walls than PVC give a better ID/OD ratio. Not suitable for SSH (subglottic suction) — the thin wall cannot accommodate an embedded suction lumen; use Twist Extract for SSH. Maximum useful life: 29 days from first use.
Angle note: The bending angle (BA) decreases with size — smaller tubes have a tighter curve (110° at size 4) and larger tubes are more open (90° at size 8–10). This reflects anatomical variation and affects tube selection for non-standard anatomy.

Tracoe (Atos Medical)
Tracoe Twist Plus (REF 312 — Cuffed Fenestrated)
Polyurethane · Double fenestration (inner + outer bend) · Double-swivel flange · Low-pressure PVC cuff
Source: Official Atos Medical Product Datasheet REF 312, © 2023/04 Coloplast A/S — directly from atosmedical.com. All measurements verified.
Clinical notes: Twist Plus has a larger OD than Twist at the same size — the fenestrated design requires additional wall material. All sizes have a 100° bend angle (more open curve than Twist at smaller sizes). Comes with both fenestrated and non-fenestrated inner cannulae. For ventilation: always use non-fenestrated IC. For phonation/valve: use fenestrated IC with cuff deflated. SSH (subglottic suction) version is available as the Twist Plus Extract series. Available sizes 07–10 in this variant.
Twist vs Twist Plus — OD difference: The Twist Plus has a consistently smaller OD than the standard Twist at the same ISO size — e.g. size 08: Twist OD = 11.4 mm vs Twist Plus OD = 10.8 mm. This is because the Twist Plus uses thinner polyurethane walls. A smaller OD means less tracheal wall pressure at the same inner diameter, which may be advantageous for long-term patients. Note however that the Twist Plus is also longer (TL) than the Twist at the same size, so verify anatomical fit.

Shiley (Medtronic)
Shiley Flexible — CN / UN Series (TaperGuard™ Cuff)
PVC · TaperGuard cuff · ISO sizing · Disposable inner cannula · Current generation
Source: Tracheostomy Sizing Chart, tracheostomyteam.org (updated 27/05/2024); United Medical Supply product specifications (individual size data confirmed); Medtronic product catalogue. These are the current generation Shiley Flexible tubes — formerly called CFS/CFN.
Clinical notes: ISO sizing — size number = inner cannula ID in mm. TaperGuard cuff provides improved seal and reduced aspiration risk. Flexible PVC softens at body temperature. The CN series is cuffed; UN is uncuffed (cuffless). Larger inner diameter compared to older Shiley Standard series (DCT/DCFS) at equivalent Jackson size. Disposable inner cannulas included.
Shiley Jackson vs ISO sizing: The older Shiley Standard (DCT/DFEN/DCFS/DCFN) series uses Jackson sizing — size 8 = 7.6 mm ID, NOT 8.0 mm. The current Flexible (CN/UN) series uses ISO sizing. Always check which series is in situ before ordering a replacement.

Bivona (ICU Medical)
Bivona TTS, Cuffless & HyperFlex Adjustable Flange
Silicone · Tight-to-shaft (TTS) or Aire-Cuf · Water-fill cuff (TTS) · Inner cannula available
Source: Tracheostomy Sizing Chart, tracheostomyteam.org (updated 27/05/2024). Note: Bivona dimensions are less commonly tabulated — verify against manufacturer IFU before clinical use.
Clinical notes: Bivona tubes are silicone — important for patients with PVC intolerance or long-term skin sensitivity. TTS (tight-to-shaft) cuff collapses completely when deflated and can be inflated to fill a larger-than-normal trachea — particularly useful for tracheomalacia or enlarged trachea. Inner cannulas are available for TTS and Aire-Cuf series. Water-filled TTS cuff should only be used in ICU/HDU where tracheal pressure can be carefully monitored. Aire-Cuf uses air and is suitable for community use.
TTS water-fill cuff: Only use in ICU/HDU. The water-fill cuff has different compliance characteristics to standard air-fill — do not assume standard cuff pressure management applies.
